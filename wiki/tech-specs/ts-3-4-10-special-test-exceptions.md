# TS 3/4.10 — Special Test Exceptions

Special Test Exceptions allow temporary relaxation of certain LCO requirements during specific reactor physics tests performed under controlled conditions.

## 3/4.10.1 — Shutdown Margin

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.10.1</div>
The shutdown margin requirement of Spec 3.1.1.1 may be suspended during PHYSICS TESTS provided the reactivity equivalent of at least two shutdown rods is available for trip insertion.
</div>

**Applicability:** Mode 2

## 3/4.10.2 — Group Height, Insertion, and Power Distribution Limits

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.10.2</div>
The group height, insertion, and power distribution limit requirements may be suspended during PHYSICS TESTS provided: SDM ≥ Spec 3.1.1.1 (except as modified by 3.10.1), RTP ≤ 85%, and trip setpoints on nuclear instruments reduced to ≤25% RTP.
</div>

## 3/4.10.3 — Physics Tests

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.10.3</div>
The MTC, minimum temp for criticality, and power distribution limit requirements may be suspended during PHYSICS TESTS provided: RTP ≤ 5%, and trip setpoints reduced.
</div>

## 3/4.10.4 — No Flow Tests

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.10.4</div>
The RCS loop requirements may be modified during no-flow tests at ≤10% RTP for measurement of isothermal and power coefficients.
</div>

---

## Bases

<span class="hi-exam">Special Test Exceptions provide flexibility for reactor physics testing performed during startup following refueling. These tests verify core nuclear parameters (MTC, rod worth, boron worth, critical boron concentration) match predictions. The compensating requirements (reduced power, additional trip setpoints) ensure adequate safety margins during testing.</span>
