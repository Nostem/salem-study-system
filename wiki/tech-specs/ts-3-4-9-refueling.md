# TS 3/4.9 — Refueling Operations

## 3/4.9.1 — Boron Concentration

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.9.1</div>
Boron concentration of the RCS, the refueling canal, and the refueling cavity shall be maintained uniform and sufficient to ensure that the more restrictive of the following reactivity conditions is met:<br>
a. keff ≤ 0.95, OR<br>
b. Boron concentration ≥ per COLR
</div>

**Applicability:** Mode 6

**Action:** With boron below required, immediately suspend CORE ALTERATIONS and positive reactivity changes. Immediately initiate boration to restore.

## 3/4.9.4 — Containment Building Penetrations

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.9.4</div>
During movement of irradiated fuel assemblies within containment, containment building penetrations shall be in their required status.
</div>

**Applicability:** During movement of irradiated fuel in containment

## 3/4.9.8 — Residual Heat Removal and Coolant Circulation

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.9.8.1 (All Water Levels)</div>
At least one RHR loop shall be OPERABLE and in operation.
</div>

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.9.8.2 (Low Water Level)</div>
Two RHR loops shall be OPERABLE and at least one in operation.
</div>

**Applicability:** Mode 6

## 3/4.9.10 — Water Level — Reactor Vessel

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-label">LCO 3.9.10</div>
At least 23 feet of water shall be maintained over the top of irradiated fuel assemblies seated in the reactor vessel during movement of fuel assemblies or control rods.
</div>

---

## Bases

<span class="hi-exam">The refueling boron concentration ensures keff ≤ 0.95 with all control rods withdrawn, providing adequate shutdown margin during refueling operations.</span> The 23-foot water level above irradiated fuel provides adequate shielding for personnel and iodine removal during a fuel handling accident.
