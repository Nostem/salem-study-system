## Option E — Clean Prose with Separator

### Q1 — RCS Low Flow / P-7 and P-8 Logic
<span style="font-size:10px;color:#6b7280;">EPE 7 EA2.08 (3.0) | Fund/Memory | New | RPS, RCP</span>

**Given:**
- Salem Unit 2 is at 25% reactor power.
- 21 RC loop flows: Ch1 89%, Ch2 89%, Ch3 92%

Which of the following describes the expected response?

- **A.** Per the ARP, manually trip the reactor, stop 21 RCP, and enter 2-EOP-TRIP-1.
- **B.** RC flows are degraded but above the RPS setpoint; power operation may continue.
- **C.** Auto trip on 2/3 low flow channels on one loop with power > P-7.
- **D.** Auto trip on 1/3 low flow channels on one loop with power > P-7.

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;">▶ Answer & Full Justification</summary>

---

**Answer: A** ✓

**A. Correct.** Two out of three channels ≤90% of design loop flow indicates degraded RCP. Since reactor power is above P-7 (10%) and below P-8 (36%), no automatic reactor trip will occur with a loss of only one RC loop low flows. However, per S2.OP-AR.ZZ-0004 for OHA D-28, with a degraded RCP flow, the RCP must be stopped, the reactor manually tripped, and enter 2-EOP-TRIP-1 since Tech Specs does not allow for three loop operation in Mode 1.

**B. Incorrect.** Plausible if the operator fails to recall the RC low flow setpoint of ≤90% design flow and believes the RCP is only degraded and does not meet the requirements for stopping the RCP.

**C. Incorrect.** Plausible if the operator believes that the conditions meet the automatic RPS reactor trip for low RC flow. Above P-7 but below P-8, one RC loop low flow does not cause an auto Rx Trip.

**D. Incorrect.** Plausible if the operator believes that 1/3 channels is the coincidence for an automatic trip. The coincidence is 2/3, and regardless, no auto trip below P-8 for single loop low flow.

*Ref: Logic dwg 221054, S2.OP-AR.ZZ-0004 (R29) | LO: NOS05RXPROT-15, Obj 12.a*

</details>
