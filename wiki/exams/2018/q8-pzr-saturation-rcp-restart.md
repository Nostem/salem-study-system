<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, with PZR Pressure at 2005 psig (2020 psia), saturation temperature is 637 &deg;F. Consequently, with PZR liquid temperature at 635 &deg;F, the PZR is NOT water saturated. For Part 2, IAW 1-EOP-TRIP-6, "To limit the pressure decrease upon RCP restart, saturated conditions should first be established in the PZR."</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, there are concerns for pressure to rise when an RCP is started when the plant is water solid. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the reason for establishing and maintaining saturation conditions in the PZR is to limit the PZR pressure increase upon RCP restart IAW 1-EOP-TRIP-6.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, since the steam tables are listed in psia (not psig), the candidate could enter the steam tables at 1990 psia and incorrectly interpolate to determine that the PZR is water saturated. For Part 2, same reasoning as A regarding pressure increase concerns.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, since the steam tables are listed in psia (not psig), the candidate could enter the steam tables at 1990 psia and incorrectly interpolate to determine that the PZR is water saturated. Part 2 is correct.</div>
