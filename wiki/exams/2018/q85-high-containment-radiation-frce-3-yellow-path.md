<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> For Part 1 and in accordance with 2-EOP-CFST-1 (Critical Safety Function Status Trees), 2-EOP-FRCE-3 entry conditions are met when R44 radiation is ≥ 2 R/HR. The earliest time that this occurs is at 14:00. For Part 2, FRCE-3 is a yellow path procedure. In accordance with OP-AA-101-111-1003 (Use Of Procedures), yellow path function restoration procedures are not required to be performed.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> For Part 1, 100 R/HR is the warning setpoint for R44A/B. The candidate could incorrectly conclude FRCE-3 entry conditions are first met when R44A/B go into warning. For Part 2, since most procedures are required to be performed when entry conditions are met, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that FRCE-3 is required to be performed when the entry conditions are met.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> For Part 1, 100 R/HR is the warning setpoint for R44A/B. The candidate could incorrectly conclude FRCE-3 entry conditions are first met when R44A/B go into warning. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, since most procedures are required to be performed when entry conditions are met, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that FRCE-3 is required to be performed when the entry conditions are met.</div>
