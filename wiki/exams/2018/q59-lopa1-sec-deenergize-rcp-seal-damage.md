<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, IAW 1-EOP-LOPA-1, prior to restoring AC power, the crew will ensure ALL SECs are de-energized. For Part 2, IAW 1-EOP-LOPA-1, preventing damage to the RCP seals is a reason that the centrifugal charging pumps are not allowed to start automatically following power restoration.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, restoring seal injection is normally desirable and will usually help protect the RCP seals. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that preventing RCS seal damage is NOT a reason for preventing auto start of the centrifugal charging pumps following power restoration.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, blocking and resetting the SECs is performed throughout the EOP Network (e.g. EOP-TRIP-1). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that EOP-LOPA-1 requires ALL SECs to be blocked and reset.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, blocking and resetting the SECs is performed throughout the EOP Network (e.g. EOP-TRIP-1). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that EOP-LOPA-1 requires ALL SECs to be blocked and reset. Part 2 is correct.</div>
