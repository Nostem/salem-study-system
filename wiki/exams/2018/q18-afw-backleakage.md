In accordance with S2.OP-PT.AF-0002, which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
S2.OP-PT.AF-0002 was REQUIRED to be performed <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> after an AFW Pump was initially shutdown in MODES 1-3.<br><br>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, IAW S2.OP-PT.AF-0002 P&L 3.1, "This procedure shall be performed 30 to 60 minutes after an Auxiliary Feedwater Pump is shutdown in Modes 1-3". For Part 2, IAW SOER 84-3 (and the AFW Lesson Plan), "backleakage of hot feedwater has disabled auxiliary feedwater (AFW) pumps due to steam binding."</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that S2.OP-PT.AF-0002 is required to be performed immediately after an AFW Pump is shutdown in Modes 1-3. For Part 2, when a pump rotates backward and the pump is started, the duration of the starting current is extended because it takes longer to get up to speed. In some cases, this extended starting current can cause the breaker for the motor to open. RCPs are specifically designed with Anti-Reverse-Rotation Devices. The candidate could incorrectly conclude that backleakage of feedwater into the AFW System can DISABLE the motor-driven AFW Pumps by causing them to rotate backwards.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that S2.OP-PT.AF-0002 is required to be performed immediately after an AFW Pump is shutdown in Modes 1-3. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, when a pump rotates backward and the pump is started, the duration of the starting current is extended. RCPs are specifically designed with Anti-Reverse-Rotation Devices. The candidate could incorrectly conclude that backleakage causes the AFW pumps to rotate backwards.</div>
