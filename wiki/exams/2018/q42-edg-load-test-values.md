<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">G2.2.21 (2.9)</span>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; Corrective Maintenance has just been completed on 2A Emergency Diesel Generator (EDG)<br>
&bull; Operations is preparing to perform a Load Test on 2A EDG as part of the Post-Maintenance Operability Retest<br><br>
In accordance with S2.OP-ST.DG-0001 (2A Diesel Generator Surveillance Test), which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
To prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power, generator load must be IMMEDIATELY raised to greater than a MINIMUM of <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> KW after the breaker is closed.<br><br>
During the Load Test, the 2A EDG may NOT exceed its <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> KW Continuous Load Rating.
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> 500 / <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> 2600</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> 500 / <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> 2750</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> 200 / <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> 2600</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> 200 / <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> 2750</div>
</div>

<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">▶ Show Answer & Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> For Part 1 and IAW S2.OP-ST.DG-0001, to prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power, generator load must be IMMEDIATELY raised to greater than a minimum of 500 KW after the breaker is closed. For Part 2 and IAW S2.OP-ST.DG-0001, during the Load Test, the 2A EDG may NOT exceed its 2600 KW Continuous Load Rating.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, S2.OP-ST.DG-0001 Precautions And Limitations states that the EDG is rated to 2750 KW for 2000 hours (2681 KW Local). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that 2750 KW is the Continuous Loading Rating on the EDG.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, S2.OP-ST.DG-0001 states that when unloading the EDG, generator load must be maintained at greater than 200 KW until the breaker is opened (to prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that to prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power, generator load must be IMMEDIATELY raised to greater than a minimum of 200 KW after the breaker is closed. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, S2.OP-ST.DG-0001 states that when unloading the EDG, generator load must be maintained at greater than 200 KW until the breaker is opened (to prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that to prevent tripping the Diesel Generator Breaker on reverse power, generator load must be IMMEDIATELY raised to greater than a minimum of 200 KW after the breaker is closed. For Part 2, S2.OP-ST.DG-0001 Precautions And Limitations states that the EDG is rated to 2750 KW for 2000 hours (2681 KW Local). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that 2750 KW is the Continuous Loading Rating on the EDG.</div>
