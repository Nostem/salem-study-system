<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; A Unit 2 Turbine Runback from 100% Reactor Power has occurred<br>
&bull; Valid ROD INSERT LIMIT LO and ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO alarms are present<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
LCO 3.1.1.1 (SHUTDOWN MARGIN - TAVG &ge; 200 &deg;F) <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> met.<br>
In accordance with the ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO Alarm Response Procedure, the crew will <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> to restore Control Rods above the Rod Insertion Limit.<br><br>
NOTE:<br>
&bull; LCO NOT met = REQUIRED ACTIONS must be performed<br>
&bull; LCO met = NO REQUIRED ACTIONS must be performed
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, with the ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO alarm present, the control rods are actually below the RIL. Consequently, SR 4.1.1.1.b (When in MODE 1 or MODE 2 with Keff greater than or equal to 1.0, in accordance with the Surveillance Frequency Control Program by verifying that control banks are within the limits in the COLR per Specification 3.1.3.5) is not met. With SR 4.1.1.1 not being met, LCO 3.1.1.1 is also NOT met. For Part 2 and in accordance with the ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO Alarm Response Procedure, the crew can perform S2.OP-SO.CVC-0008 (Rapid Boration) ONLY to restore Control Rods above the Rod Insertion Limit.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, raising turbine power will cause RCS TAVG to lower which would cause Control Rods to automatically withdraw. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO Alarm Response Procedure allows the crew to raise turbine power to restore Control Rods above the Rod Insertion Limit.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that SR 4.1.1.1 only requires a calculation of Shutdown Margin (SDM) and LCO 3.1.1.1 is met until the SDM calculation confirms that SDM is not adequate. For Part 2, raising turbine power will cause RCS TAVG to lower which would cause Control Rods to automatically withdraw. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the ROD INSERT LIMIT LO-LO Alarm Response Procedure allows the crew to raise turbine power to restore Control Rods above the Rod Insertion Limit.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that SR 4.1.1.1 only requires a calculation of Shutdown Margin (SDM) and LCO 3.1.1.1 is met until the SDM calculation confirms that SDM is not adequate. Part 2 is correct.</div>
