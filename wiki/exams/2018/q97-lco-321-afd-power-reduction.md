<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; Unit 2 is at 95% Power<br><br>
At time 15:30<br>
&bull; The CRS has declared LCO 3.2.1 (AXIAL FLUX DIFFERENCE (AFD)) NOT met<br><br>
In accordance with LCO 3.2.1, which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
The crew is REQUIRED to reduce THERMAL POWER to less than <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> % of RATED THERMAL POWER by 15:45 (15 minutes after LCO 3.2.1 was NOT met).<br>
The LCO 3.2.1 REQUIRED power reduction will ensure <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> limit as specified in the COLR is NOT exceeded.
</div>

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> For Part 1 and IAW LCO 3.2.1, with Reactor Power at 95% power and LCO 3.2.1 NOT met at 15:30, the crew is required to reduce THERMAL POWER to less than 90% of RATED THERMAL POWER by 15:45 (within 15 minutes). For Part 2 and IAW LCO Bases, the LCO 3.2.1 REQUIRED power reduction will ensure F<sub>Q</sub>(Z) (Heat Flux Hot Channel Factor) limit as specified in the COLR is NOT exceeded.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, the LCO Bases for the Power Distribution LCOs (LCO 3.2.x) are designed to protect three different hot channel factors (Heat Flux Hot Channel, Nuclear Enthalpy Rise Hot Channel Factor and Radial Peaking Factor). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that LCO 3.2.1 is designed to prevent exceeding the Radial Peaking Factor.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1 and IAW LCO 3.2.1, once reactor power is between 90%-50%, the crew must then reduce power to less than 50% in the next 30 minutes. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that when LCO 3.2.1 was first not met (with RX Power at 95%), the crew is required to reduce THERMAL POWER to less than 50% of RATED THERMAL POWER by 15:45 (within 15 minutes). Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1 and IAW LCO 3.2.1, once reactor power is between 90%-50%, the crew must then reduce power to less than 50% in the next 30 minutes. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that when LCO 3.2.1 was first not met (with RX Power at 95%), the crew is required to reduce THERMAL POWER to less than 50% of RATED THERMAL POWER by 15:45 (within 15 minutes). For Part 2, the LCO Bases for the Power Distribution LCOs (LCO 3.2.x) are designed to protect three different hot channel factors (Heat Flux Hot Channel, Nuclear Enthalpy Rise Hot Channel Factor and Radial Peaking Factor). Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that LCO 3.2.1 is designed to prevent exceeding the Radial Peaking Factor.</div>
