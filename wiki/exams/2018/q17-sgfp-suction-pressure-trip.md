<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 is at 100% Reactor Power and stable<br><br>
At time 09:00:00<br>
- The suction pressure transmitter for the 22 SGFP FAILS to 200 psig<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
The 22 SGFP will TRIP <span class="blank">_(1)_</span>.<br>
After 22 SGFP has tripped, Reactor Power will stabilize at approximately <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> %.
</div>

<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> <span class="blank">(1)</span> IMMEDIATELY (at 09:00:00) &nbsp;&nbsp; <span class="blank">(2)</span> 66</div>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, the SGFP will trip when suction pressure is less than 215 psig for 10 seconds. Consequently, given the question stem, 09:00:10 is the LATEST time that the 22 SGFP TRIPS. For Part 2, once 22 SGFP trips, a turbine runback to 66% will be generated. Since Reactor Power follows steam demand, Reactor Power will stabilize at approximately 66%.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> For Part 1, there is an instantaneous SGFP trip on low suction pressure (&lt; 190 psig). The candidate could incorrectly conclude that the SGFP will trip instantaneously at 09:00:00. For Part 2, there are only two SGFPs. The candidate could incorrectly conclude that each SGFP provides 50% total flow and that a turbine runback will occur to 50%.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> For Part 1, there is an instantaneous SGFP trip on low suction pressure (&lt; 190 psig). The candidate could incorrectly conclude that the SGFP will trip instantaneously at 09:00:00. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, there are only two SGFPs. The candidate could incorrectly conclude that each SGFP provides 50% total flow and that a turbine runback will occur to 50%.</div>
