<span style="color:#60a5fa;font-size:14px;font-weight:600;">Q99 — Liquid Radwaste Release / 2R18 Inoperable</span>
<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">G2.3.6 (3.8)</span>
</div>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; The CRS is reviewing a release permit for a release from 21 CVCS Monitor Tank<br>
&bull; 2R18 (Waste Disposal Liquid Rad Monitor) has failed its source check<br><br>
In accordance with S2.OP-SO.WL-0001 (Release Of Radioactive Liquid Waste From 21 CVCS Monitor Tank), which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>
The CRS <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> approve the release permit <span class="blank">_(2)_</span>.
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> (1) can (2) provided a second sample was drawn, analyzed, and calculations were second verified prior to the release</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> (1) can (2) provided continuous effluent sampling is conducted throughout the liquid waste discharge</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> (1) can NOT (2) until ONLY the 2R18 is repaired and returned to service</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> (1) can NOT (2) until 2R18 is repaired and returned to service and after a second sample was drawn, analyzed, and calculations were second verified prior to the release</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">▶ Show Answer & Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> IAW S2.OP-SO.WL-0001, if 2R18 is inoperable, then the CRS can approve the release permit provided a second sample was drawn, analyzed, and calculations were second verified prior to the release.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, since 2R18 provides continuous monitoring during the release, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the effluent must be continuously sampled during the discharge.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. The candidate could incorrectly conclude that the release permit can not be approved until ONLY the 2R18 is repaired and returned to service.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Since the 21 CVCS Monitor Tank can be discharged into the Unit 1 CW system, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the Unit 1 R18 can be aligned to monitor the 21 CVCS Monitor Tank. Consequently, the candidate could then incorrectly determine that the release permit can not be approved until 2R18 is repaired and returned to service or until the Unit 1 R18 can be source checked and aligned to monitor the release.</div>
