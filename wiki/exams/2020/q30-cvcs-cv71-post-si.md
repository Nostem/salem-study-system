&bull; A <span class="hi-exam">Reactor Trip and Safety Injection (SI) have automatically actuated</span>.<br><br>
The Reactor Operator is <span class="hi">throttling closed</span> on 2CV71, Seal Pressure Control Valve.<br><br>
What is the effect on Charging Pump discharge pressure and charging flow to the RCS?<br><br>
Charging Pump discharge pressure will...
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> rise and total Charging flow will remain constant.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> rise and total Charging flow will lower.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> remain constant and RCP Seal Injection flow will rise.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> remain constant and Charging flow will remain constant.</div>

<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">&#9654; Show Answer & Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; D. Correct.</strong> CV-68 &amp; 69, Charging Header Isolation Valves go closed on an SI signal and are in series with the CV-71 flow path, therefore no change to charging pump discharge pressure or flow occur.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Plausible because during normal power alignment, throttling the CV-71 will result in a rise in charging discharge pressure and total charging flow will remain constant. Flow will increase to the RCP seals, while charging flow to the regenerative heat exchanger will lower. Incorrect because CV-68 &amp; 69, Charging Header Isolation Valves go closed on an SI signal and are in series with the CV-71 flow path. The second part is correct, total charging flow will remain constant.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> Plausible because during normal power alignment, throttling the CV-71 will result in a rise in charging discharge pressure. Additionally the candidate may confuse total charging flow with charging flow to the regenerative heat exchanger which will lower. Incorrect because CV-68 &amp; 69, Charging Header Isolation Valves go closed on an SI signal and are in series with the CV-71 flow path.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> The first part is correct, charging discharge pressure will remain constant as the CV-68 &amp; 69, Charging Header Isolation Valves go closed on an SI signal and are in series with the CV-71 flow path. The second part is plausible because during a normal power alignment, closing down on the CV-71 would increase seal injection flow.</div>
