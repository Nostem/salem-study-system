<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">086000A1.05 (2.9)</span>
</div>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
<ul style="margin:0;padding-left:20px;">
<li>Unit 2 is at 100% Power.</li>
</ul>
<br>
The following sequence of events occurs:<br>
<ul style="margin:0;padding-left:20px;">
<li>OHA A-15, FIRE PMP 1/2 RUN in alarm</li>
<li>OHA A-23, FIRE PUMP 1/2 TRBL in alarm.</li>
<li>BOTH Fire Pumps are running.</li>
<li>NO other Fire Protection System alarms are present.</li>
<li>Fire protection reports NO fire system actuations.</li>
<li>Fire main header pressure is 135 psig and stable.</li>
</ul>
<br>
Which ONE of the following describes the cause for the above conditions?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> Fire Protection Jockey Pump tripped.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> Major Fire Protection System pipe rupture.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> Loss of Normal AC power to BOTH Fire Pump Battery Chargers.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> A momentary (1 second) drop in Fire Protection header pressure to 70 psig.</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">▶ Show Answer & Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ C. Correct.</strong> The diesel driven fire pumps are normally aligned in standby and normally start on low header pressure signals of <85# and time delayed <75# respectively.  But the system configuration also includes an independent battery that will automatically start the fire pumps during a loss of normal AC power.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Plausible because the Jockey Pump normally maintains fire protection header pressure between 110-120 psig and the candidate may believe that the trip of the jockey pump is an automatic start signal for the fire pumps.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Plausible in that since the fire pumps start at <85# and <70# with a time delay that a large pipe rupture could have caused system pressure to lower to the start setpoints, but that the pumps were presently able to maintain 135# based on the size of the leak.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Plausible in that since the fire pumps start at <85# and <70# with a time delay that a large pipe rupture could have caused system pressure to lower to the start setpoints.  Incorrect as the start for the #2 Fire Pump includes a time delay and therefore a momentary (1 sec) pressure drop would not have started both pumps.</div>
