<strong>Given:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 has experienced a Reactor Trip and Safety Injection due to a Large Break LOCA.<br>
- RWST Level has lowered to less than 15.2 feet on 2/4 RWST Level Channels.<br>
<br>
With NO operator action, which choice describes AUTOMATIC actions that will occur based on the above RWST Level?
</div>

<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> 21 & 22 CS36s, RHR Discharge to Containment Spray Header OPEN.</div>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; B. Correct.</strong> Normal 100% Power ECCS Lineup would have both the SJ113 valves "armed" for semi-automatic switchover signal.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because the operator subsequently manually "arms" the SJ44s if sump level is > 62% and the valves will then automatically open.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because the candidate may remember the Unit 1 valve interlock that requires the RH4 to be closed prior to opening the SJ44 and believe that these valves are already "armed".</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because the CS36 valve will get manually manipulated depending on the available RHR pump to ensure continued containment spray header flow. These valves are manipulated at LO-LO RWST Level however and also manually.</div>
