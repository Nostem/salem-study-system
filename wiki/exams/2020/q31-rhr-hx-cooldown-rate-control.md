<span style="color:#60a5fa;font-size:14px;font-weight:600;">Q31 — RHR HX Cooldown Rate Control</span>
<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">005000K6.03 (2.5)</span>
</div>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br><br>
&bull; Unit 2 is in MODE 5.<br>
&bull; 21 RHR HX Loop is providing Shutdown Cooling with a cooldown rate of 20 &deg;F/hr.<br>
&bull; 21RH18, RHR HX Flow Control Valve, is throttled at 40 % to maintain total flow at 2500 gpm.<br>
&bull; 2RH20, RHR HX Bypass Valve, is throttled at 30 % open.<br><br>
Subsequently the following occurs:<br><br>
&bull; Foreign material dropped in the Refueling Cavity is introduced into the RHR system causing partial blockage on the 21 RHR HX tubes.<br>
&bull; Cooldown rate has lowered to 5 &deg;F/hr.<br><br>
Based on the above conditions, in order to maintain a 20 &deg;F/hr cooldown rate, the operator will be required to _______. (Assume decay heat load remains constant)
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> Lower the demand on the 21RH18 and Lower the demand on the 2RH20.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> Lower the demand on the 21RH18 and Raise the demand on the 2RH20.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> Raise the demand on the 21RH18 and Raise the demand on the 2RH20.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> Raise the demand on the 21RH18 and Lower the demand on the 2RH20.</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">&#9654; Show Answer &amp; Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; D. Correct.</strong> Raising the controller demand for the 21RHR18 will increase flow through the RHR HX and lowering the controller demand for the 2RH20 will decrease the RHR HX bypass flow.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because the candidate may confuse system configuration and valve demand operation and believe that this will increase flow through the heat exchanger.</div>

Plausible because the candidate may confuse system configuration and valve demand operation and believe that this will increase flow through the heat exchanger.</div>

Plausible because the candidate may confuse system configuration and valve demand operation and believe that this will increase flow through the heat exchanger.</div>
