<strong>Initial Conditions:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 experienced a Large Break LOCA and an Automatic Safety Injection (SI).<br>
- All available equipment started following SI signal.<br>
- An ALERT was declared based on EAL# RB1.L, Loss of the Reactor Coolant System Barrier.<br>
- The Emergency Core Cooling System (ECCS) is currently aligned for Cold Leg Recirculation IAW EOP-LOCA-3.<br>
- The crew is responding to the following alarms and indications:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;- OHA C-26, 21 RHR SUMP OVRFLO<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;- Auxiliary Typewriter alarm point; 21 RHR Sump Pump start<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;- 2R41D, Plant Vent Radiation Monitor, indicating 3.18E+05 &micro;Ci/sec and rising<br>
- The crew responded to the alarms/indications by closing the 21SJ44, Containment Sump Suction Valve, to isolate the leak but the valve failed to close.<br>
- The OCC is briefing a field team to investigate the breaker associated for the 21SJ44 valve.<br>
- The 2R44A and 2R44B, Containment High Range Radiation Monitors, are currently reading 305 R/Hr.<br>
- The 2R41D, Plant Vent Radiation Monitor, is currently reading 5.12E+6 &micro;Ci/sec and rising.<br>
- Met Tower data indicates wind from 180 degrees at 5 mph.<br><br>
<strong>Initiating Cue:</strong><br>
You are the Emergency Coordinator (EC).<br>
CLASSIFY the event AND COMPLETE / APPROVE the ICMF IAW the applicable procedure.<br>
THIS IS A TIME CRITICAL JPM.

<div style="margin-top:12px;padding:10px;background:#1a1400;border-left:3px solid #fbbf24;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color:#fbbf24;">Fission Product Barrier Assessment:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">
<strong>RCS Barrier (5 pts):</strong> RB1.L — Loss of RCS barrier. Large Break LOCA with ECCS in cold leg recirculation (EOP-LOCA-3) confirms RCS integrity has been lost.<br>
<strong>Fuel Barrier (5 pts):</strong> FB2.L — Loss of Fuel barrier. Containment High Range Radiation Monitors (2R44A/B) reading 305 R/Hr indicates significant fuel damage.<br>
<strong>Containment Barrier (3 pts):</strong> CB2.L or CB3.L — Loss or potential loss of Containment barrier. 21SJ44 (Containment Sump Suction Valve) failed to close, creating an unisolable leak path outside containment. 2R41D (Plant Vent Rad Monitor) at 5.12E+6 &micro;Ci/sec and rising confirms radioactivity is being released.<br><br>
Total: 13 points = <strong>General Emergency</strong> (escalated from ALERT).
</span>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:12px;padding:10px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color:#22c55e;">Key Decision Point:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">The candidate must escalate from the existing ALERT to a General Emergency by performing a three-barrier assessment using the ECG books. The critical recognition is that all three fission product barriers are lost or potentially lost: (1) RCS lost from the LBLOCA, (2) fuel cladding lost per high containment radiation readings, and (3) containment lost/potentially lost because the 21SJ44 valve failed to close, allowing an unisolable leak path outside containment with confirmed elevated Plant Vent radiation. A release IS in progress (2R41D elevated and rising). The ICMF must correctly identify this as a GE with a release in progress and use Attachment 4 (EP-SA-325-F4).</span>
</div>
