<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; The crew is performing 2-EOP-TRIP-4 (NATURAL CIRCULATION COOLDOWN)<br>
&bull; Wide Range Loop 22 T HOT has failed low<br><br>
At time 12:00<br>
&bull; OHA B-11 "2B VTL INSTR BUS INVRT FAIL" actuates<br>
&bull; ALL Channel II indications are lost<br>
&bull; The crew enters S2.OP-AB.115-0002 (LOSS OF 2B 115V VITAL INSTRUMENT BUS)<br><br>
At time 12:30<br>
&bull; The crew has just completed the RCS Stabilization Steps of 2-EOP-TRIP-4 and is now preparing to Cooldown and Depressurize the RCS<br>
&bull; ALL Channel II indications are still lost<br><br>
In accordance with 2-EOP-TRIP-4, which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>
The crew will transition to <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> if the required RCS Cooldown Rate is greater than a MINIMUM of <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> &deg;F / Hour.<br><br>
NOTE:<br>
&bull; 2-EOP-TRIP-5 (NATURAL CIRCULATION RAPID COOLDOWN WITH RVLIS)<br>
&bull; 2-EOP-TRIP-6 (NATURAL CIRCULATION RAPID COOLDOWN WITHOUT RVLIS)

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; C. Correct.</strong> IAW 2-EOP-TRIP-4, if RVLIS is available then the crew will transition to 2-EOP-TRIP-5 (NATURAL CIRCULATION RAPID COOLDOWN WITH RVLIS) if the required RCS Cooldown Rate is greater than a MINIMUM of 25 &deg;F / Hour. Loop 22 THOT provides input to Train A of RVLIS. 2B Vital Instrument Bus provides the power for RVLIS Train A Microprocessor Panel. Consequently, ONLY Train A of RVLIS is inoperable at 12:30. Train B of RVLIS not affected.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could correctly identify that Train A of RVLIS is inoperable when 2B Vital Instrument Bus de-energized. The candidate could then incorrectly conclude that Train B of RVLIS becomes invalid with Wide Range Loop 22 THOT has failed low. The candidate would then incorrectly conclude that both trains of RVLIS are inoperable and performance of 2-EOP-TRIP-6 (NATURAL CIRCULATION RAPID COOLDOWN WITHOUT RVLIS) is warranted. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could correctly identify that Train A of RVLIS is inoperable when 2B Vital Instrument Bus de-energized. The candidate could then incorrectly conclude that Train B of RVLIS becomes invalid with Wide Range Loop 22 THOT has failed low. The candidate would then incorrectly conclude that both trains of RVLIS are inoperable and performance of 2-EOP-TRIP-6 (NATURAL CIRCULATION RAPID COOLDOWN WITHOUT RVLIS) is warranted. For Part 2, other EOPs and SOPs have maximum cooldown rates of 50 &deg;F. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude transition to 2-EOP-TRIP-5 or 2-EOP-TRIP-6 is warranted when the required RCS Cooldown Rate is greater than a MINIMUM of 50 &deg;F / Hour.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, other EOPs and SOPs have maximum cooldown rates of 50 &deg;F. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude transition to 2-EOP-TRIP-5 or 2-EOP-TRIP-6 is warranted when the required RCS Cooldown Rate is greater than a MINIMUM of 50 &deg;F / Hour.</div>
