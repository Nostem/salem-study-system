<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; John Doe has received 1000 mrem routine cumulative TEDE this year<br>
&bull; A SITE AREA EMERGENCY has just been declared<br>
&bull; John Doe is preparing to go into an area to mitigate the casualty<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;o The area has a general dose rate of 1000 mrem / hour<br><br>
In accordance with RP-AA-203 (Exposure Control and Authorization) and NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304 (OPERATIONAL SUPPORT CENTER (OSC) RADIATION PROTECTION RESPONSE), which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>
John Doe can work in the area for a MAXIMUM of ___ minutes without exceeding his ADMINISTRATIVE dose limits for the year.
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 60</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 180</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> 210</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> 240</div>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; C. Correct.</strong> IAW RP-AA-203, the Administrative Dose Limit for routine work is 2000 mrem TEDE. When the Site Area Emergency is declared, then IAW NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304, the administrative dose limit is automatically raised to 4500 mrem. Consequently, the worker can work (4500 - 1000) / 1000 = 3.5 hours or 210 minutes in the area.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> 60 Minutes would be the stay time if the candidate used the normal administrative limit of 2000 mrem.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> 180 Minutes would be the stay time if the candidate incorrectly concluded that the administrative dose limit is automatically raised to 4000 mrem.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> 240 Minutes would be the stay time if the candidate incorrectly concluded that the administrative dose limit is automatically raised to 5000 mrem.</div>
