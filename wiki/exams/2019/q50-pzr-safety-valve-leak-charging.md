<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">APE: 008 AK2.03 (2.5)</span>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
&bull; Unit 2 is at 100% Reactor Power<br>
&bull; 23 Charging Pump is in service<br>
&bull; Charging Flow is 88 GPM<br><br>
At time 11:25:00<br>
&bull; Pressurizer pressure starts to lower due to 2PR3 (PZR SAFETY VLV) seat leakage<br><br>
At time 11:27:00 (2 Minutes Later)<br>
&bull; Pressurizer pressure is 2100 psig and lowering<br><br>
At time 11:29:00 (4 Minutes Later)<br>
&bull; Pressurizer pressure is 1910 psig and lowering<br>
&bull; An AUTOMATIC Reactor Trip occurs<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>
At 11:27, Charging Flow is <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> 88 GPM.<br>
At 11:29, the FIRST-OUT alarm indicates that the Reactor tripped on <span class="blank">_(2)_</span>.
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> (1) greater than (2) OT&Delta;T</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> (1) greater than (2) OP&Delta;T</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> (1) less than (2) OT&Delta;T</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> (1) less than (2) OP&Delta;T</div>

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; A. Correct.</strong> For Part 1, with seat leakage of the PZR Safety Valve, actual PZR level will lower below program level. Consequently, the master flow controller will automatically raise charging flow in an effort to raise PZR level. Consequently, at 11:27, Charging Flow is greater than 88 GPM (the original charging flow prior to the malfunction). For Part 2, the OT&Delta;T Reactor Trip setpoint is variable and will be automatically lowered as RCS pressure lowers. Consequently, at 11:29, the FIRST-OUT alarm indicates that the Reactor tripped on OT&Delta;T.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, OP&Delta;T is also a variable Reactor Trip setpoint that changes as AFD changes. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the OP&Delta;T Reactor Trip setpoint changes as RCS pressure changes. This would then cause the candidate to incorrectly conclude that at 11:29, the FIRST-OUT alarm indicates that the Reactor tripped on OP&Delta;T.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> For Part 1, since the PZR Safety Valves are located on top of the PZR, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that PZR level actually rises due to the PZR Safety Valve seat leakage. When PZR level rises above program level, the master flow controller will actually lower charging flow below 88 GPM (the original charging flow prior to the malfunction). Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> For Part 1, since the PZR Safety Valves are located on top of the PZR, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that PZR level actually rises due to the PZR Safety Valve seat leakage. When PZR level rises above program level, the master flow controller will actually lower charging flow below 88 GPM (the original charging flow prior to the malfunction). For Part 2, OP&Delta;T is also a variable Reactor Trip setpoint that changes as AFD changes. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that the OP&Delta;T Reactor Trip setpoint changes as RCS pressure changes. This would then cause the candidate to incorrectly conclude that at 11:29, the FIRST-OUT alarm indicates that the Reactor tripped on OP&Delta;T.</div>
