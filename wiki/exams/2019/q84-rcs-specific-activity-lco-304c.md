In accordance with Technical Specifications, which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; B. Correct.</strong> For Part 1 and IAW LCO 3.4.9, the LCO 3.4.9 specific activity limit for DOSE EQUIVALENT I-131 is less or equal to a MAXIMUM of 1.0 &micro;Ci/gram. For Part 2, LCO 3.0.4.c gives an allowance for LCO 3.4.9 to allow going up in MODE or other specified condition when LCO 3.4.9 is NOT met.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, the candidate could fail to recognize or misunderstand the LCO 3.0.4.c exemption for LCO 3.4.9 and incorrectly conclude that the crew is NOT permitted to raise TAVG &ge; 500 &deg;F while RCS activity is still above the LCO 3.4.9 limit.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, 100 &micro;Ci/gram is another limit specified in LCO 3.4.9. For Part 2, the candidate could fail to recognize or misunderstand the LCO 3.0.4.c exemption for LCO 3.4.9 and incorrectly conclude that the crew is NOT permitted to raise TAVG &ge; 500 &deg;F while RCS activity is still above the LCO 3.4.9 limit.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, 100 &micro;Ci/gram is another limit specified in LCO 3.4.9. Part 2 is correct.</div>
