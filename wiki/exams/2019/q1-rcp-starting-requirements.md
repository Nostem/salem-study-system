<strong>Given:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 is in MODE 5<br>
- ALL RCPs are stopped<br>
- The crew is preparing to start 23 RCP in accordance with S2.OP-SO.RC-0001 (REACTOR COOLANT PUMP OPERATION)<br><br>
Considering ONLY the following plant conditions below:<br>
1. Pressurizer level is less than 92%<br>
2. ALL Steam Generator secondary side temperatures are less than 50°F above any RCS Cold Leg temperature<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes the statement below?<br><br>
With one or more RCS Cold Leg temperatures less than or equal to 312°F, <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> conditions shall be met before starting 23 RCP.<br><br>
Starting 23 RCP when the start criteria are NOT met will result in <span class="blank">_(2)_</span>.
</div>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> IAW S2.OP-SO.RC-0001 (REACTOR COOLANT PUMP OPERATION) Limitations, with one or more RCS Cold Leg temperatures less than or equal to 312°F, at least one of conditions shall be met before starting 23 RCP. Starting 23 RCP when the start criteria are NOT met will result in an RCS pressure transient caused by energy addition from the secondary system.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that both conditions must be met to start the 23 RCP. For Part 2, protecting the RCP motor is a valid concern and there are design features of the RCPs and starting duty requirements which protect the RCP motor. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that starting 23 RCP when the start criteria are NOT met will result in degradation of the RCP motor due to a larger motor starting current.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that both conditions must be met to start the 23 RCP. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, protecting the RCP motor is a valid concern and there are design features of the RCPs and starting duty requirements which protect the RCP motor. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that starting 23 RCP when the start criteria are NOT met will result in degradation of the RCP motor due to a larger motor starting current.</div>
