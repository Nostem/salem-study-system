<strong>Given:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 Pressurizer is water solid<br><br>
At time 12:00<br>
- The crew is performing a Pressurizer heatup in accordance with S2.OP-SO.RC-0002 (VACUUM REFILL OF THE RCS) Section 5.4, "Establishing Pressurizer Steam Bubble Without A Vacuum In RCS"<br><br>
At time 13:00<br>
- The crew has opened 2PR1 (PZR PORV) for Pressurizer degassing in accordance with S2.OP-SO.RC-0002 Section 5.4<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes the statements below?<br><br>
In accordance with LCO 3.4.10.2 (Pressurizer), the MAXIMUM allowable Pressurizer heatup rate is <span class="blank">_(1)_</span> &deg;F per hour.<br><br>
During the Pressurizer degassing, the crew will open and close 2PR1 to maintain PRT pressure less than a MAXIMUM of <span class="blank">_(2)_</span> psig.
</div>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> For Part 1 and IAW LCO 3.4.10.2, the MAXIMUM allowable Pressurizer heatup rate is 100°F per hour. For Part 2 and IAW S2.OP-SO.RC-0002 Section 5.4, during the Pressurizer degassing, the crew will open and close 2PR1 to maintain PRT pressure less than a MAXIMUM of 10 psig.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. Part 1 is correct. For Part 2, 25 psig is a pressure that is below the PRT rupture disc setpoint of 100 psig and close to the actual limit. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that S2.OP-SO.RC-0002 Section 5.4 requires PRT pressure to be maintained less than a maximum of 25 psig.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, LCO 3.4.10.2 allows a maximum pressurizer cooldown of 200°F per hour. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that LCO 3.4.10.2 allows a maximum pressurizer heatup of 200°F per hour. Part 2 is correct.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect but plausible. For Part 1, LCO 3.4.10.2 allows a maximum pressurizer cooldown of 200°F per hour. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that LCO 3.4.10.2 allows a maximum pressurizer heatup of 200°F per hour. For Part 2, 25 psig is a pressure that is below the PRT rupture disc setpoint of 100 psig and close to the actual limit. Consequently, the candidate could incorrectly conclude that S2.OP-SO.RC-0002 Section 5.4 requires PRT pressure to be maintained less than a maximum of 25 psig.</div>
