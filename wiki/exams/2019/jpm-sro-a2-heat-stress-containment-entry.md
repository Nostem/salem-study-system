<strong>Location:</strong> Classroom (Administrative)<br>
<strong>Time-Critical:</strong> No<br>
<strong>Alternate Path:</strong> No<br>
<strong>Estimated Time:</strong> 10 minutes<br><br>
<strong>Initial Conditions:</strong><br>
- Unit 2 is at 100% power.<br>
- 2PS3, PZR Spray Valve, is operating erratically and a decision has been made to enter containment to isolate the 2PS3 by closing the manual isolation valve (2PS28).<br>
- The 2PS28 is located inside the PZR housing (dog house), upper elevation, and is known to be very physically demanding on the operators to operate. Two operators will be required to simultaneously operate the valve using a valve wrench during the entire valve operation. Operators will also have to climb ladders to access the 2PS28 in the dog house.<br>
- The Shift Manager needs to determine the amount of resources needed to close this valve given the Heat Stress conditions.<br>
- Rad Pro has provided a Wet Bulb Globe Thermometer (WBGT) temperature of 89 F for the work area.<br>
- Rad Pro has determined that operators will need to wear double PCs.<br>
- Cooling Garments will not be used.<br><br>
<strong>Initiating Cue:</strong><br>
You are the Unit 2 Control Room Supervisor.<br>
DETERMINE the following using SA-AA-111, Heat Stress Control for the two workers:<br>
1. What is the Stay Time?<br>
2. Assume the task will need more time. What is the Maximum Extended Stay Time?<br>
3. Assume the task will NOT use the Maximum Extended Stay Time. What is the Recovery Time?
</div>

<div style="padding:10px 14px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #34d399;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:14px;font-size:13px;">
<strong style="color:#34d399;">Task Standard:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">Determines: Stay Time = 20 mins, Maximum Extended Stay Time = 30 mins, and Recovery Time = 60 mins.</span>
</div>

<td style="padding:6px;color:#9ca3af;">CRS calculates Maximum Extended Stay Time (1.5 x ST): 1.5 x 20 mins = 30 mins. Check Times cannot extend beyond 1.5 times the established Stay Time.</td>

<td style="padding:6px;color:#c8cdd5;">Determine Recovery Time</td>
<td style="padding:6px;color:#9ca3af;">Stay Time (ST) = 20 mins. Actual Work Time = Stay Time. Recovery Time = (20/20) x 60 = 60 mins.</td>

<div style="margin-top:12px;padding:10px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color:#22c55e;">Key Decision Point:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">The applicant must correctly classify the work rate as HIGH based on the physical demands described in the initial conditions (valve wrench, climbing ladders, two operators simultaneously operating a valve in the PZR dog house). This drives the Stay Time determination. With WBGT of 89 F + 9 F clothing adjustment = 98 F effective WBGT, and High work rate, Attachment 3 yields a Stay Time of 20 minutes. The Maximum Extended Stay Time is simply 1.5x the Stay Time, and Recovery Time uses the formula (Actual Work Time / Stay Time) x 60 minutes.</span>
</div>
