<strong>Alternate Path:</strong> No<br>
<strong>Estimated Time:</strong> 8 minutes<br><br>
<strong>Initial Conditions:</strong><br>
- Salem 2 is at 100% power.<br>
- The control room is responding to valid indications of a Steam Generator tube leak in 23 Steam Generator IAW S2.OP-AB.SG-0001.<br>
- The RO reports that PZR level and RCS pressure are lowering rapidly.<br>
- The CRS implemented the CAS of AB.SG-0001 to trip the Reactor and initiate Safety Injection.<br>
- The crew is currently implementing 2-EOP-SGTR-1.<br>
- 23 MS167 failed to close from the control room and the crew has initiated close on 21, 22, and 24 MS167's.<br>
- Following the closing of the 21, 22, & 24 MS167's, the control room suddenly hears a loud roaring noise.<br>
- The PO reports that 23 SG pressure is 850 psig and lowering rapidly and steam flows are approx. 12% and rising. All other SG pressures are stable around 995 psig with 0-1% steam flow.<br>
- Met Tower data indicates wind from 105 degrees at 5 mph.<br><br>
<strong>Initiating Cue:</strong><br>
You are the Unit 2 Control Room Supervisor (CRS).<br>
CLASSIFY the event AND COMPLETE the ICMF IAW the applicable attachment.<br>
The JPM will stop when you have submitted the ICMF to your evaluator.<br>
THIS IS A TIME CRITICAL JPM.
</div>

<strong style="color:#22c55e;">Key Decision Point:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">The applicant must recognize the fission product barrier status: (1) RCS barrier is lost — SGTR is a direct breach of the RCS pressure boundary (RB3.L = 5 pts), and (2) Containment barrier is lost or potentially lost — the stuck-open 23 MS167 (MSIV) creates an uncontrolled release path from the RCS through the ruptured 23 SG tube, out the open MSIV to atmosphere (CB3.L or CB4.L = 3 pts). Total of 8 points meets the SAE threshold. The loud roaring noise with rapidly lowering 23 SG pressure and rising steam flow confirms the stuck-open MSIV is passing steam to atmosphere. A release IS in progress via this path.</span>
