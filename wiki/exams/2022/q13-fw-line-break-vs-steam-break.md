<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br><br>
• Unit 2 is at 100% Power.<br>
• A Main Feedwater Line Break inside containment occurs on 21 Steam Generator.<br><br>
Which ONE of the following completes both statements?<br><br>
1. On a Main Feedwater Line Break, Narrow Range levels in the 21 SG will lower <span class="blank">__(1)__</span> when compared to a Steam Line Break.<br>
2. Immediately following the reactor trip, the RCS cooldown rate will be <span class="blank">__(2)__</span> when compared to an equivalent size Steam Line Break.
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 1) slower<br>2) less</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 1) slower<br>2) more</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> 1) faster<br>2) more</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> 1) faster<br>2) less</div>

<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">&#9654; Show Answer &amp; Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; D. Correct.</strong> A Main Feedwater Line Break is expected to generate a Low-Low SG Level reactor trip due to the loss of feedwater at 100% power. Post Trip, RCS Tavg will rise due to lowering feed flow to the affected SG causing a decrease in heat transfer in that SG. A FW line break creates less cooling on the RCS because the loss of FW from the SG and out the break does not involve the removal of latent heat as occurs in a steam break.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> First part is incorrect. SG levels will lower faster during a feedwater line break. Second part is correct. Plausible because the operator may incorrectly determine that on a feed line break SG levels will lower slower compared to a steam line break.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> First part is incorrect. SG levels will lower faster during a feedwater line break. Second part is incorrect. Feedwater line break will have a lesser effect on RCS cooling. Plausible because the operator may incorrectly determine that on a feed line break SG levels will lower slower compared to a steam line break.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> First part is correct. Second part is incorrect. Feedwater line break will have a lesser effect on RCS cooling. Plausible because the operator may incorrectly determine how feed line break will effect RCS cooldown.</div>
