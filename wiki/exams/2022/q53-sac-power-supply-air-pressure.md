<span style="color:#60a5fa;font-size:14px;font-weight:600;">Q53 — SAC Power Supply &amp; Control Air Pressure</span>
<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">078 K2.01 (2.7)</span>
</div>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br><br>
• Both Units are at 100% Power.<br>
• #1 Station Air Compressor (SAC) is running.<br>
• #2 SAC is in AUTO and next to load.<br>
• #3 SAC is C/T for maintenance.<br>
• There is no air usage at either unit's condensate polisher system.<br>
• Control Air headers A and B on both units are indicating 105 psig.<br><br>
Subsequently, the following occurs;<br>
• Unit 1 reactor trips and the 4KV Group Busses fail to automatically transfer resulting in ALL 4KV Group Busses being de-energized.<br>
• Unit 2 is NOT affected.<br><br>
15 minutes later, which ONE of the following would be an expected air pressure indication for both control air headers on Units 1 and 2?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 60 psig and lowering rapidly.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 80 psig and lowering slowly.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> Stable at approximately 85-88 psig.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> Stable at approximately 100 psig.</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">&#9654; Show Answer &amp; Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; D. Correct.</strong> Instrument air is supplied from one of three station air compressors. Unit 1 has two station air compressors (SAC), #1 SAC supplied from 1H 4KV group bus and #3 SAC supplied from 1G 4KV group buses and Unit 2 has one SAC (#2 SAC) supplied from the 2G 4KV group bus. Therefore, with a loss of the 4KV Group Buses on Unit 1 only, the #2 SAC on Unit 2 would start (if not already running) and supply station air to both units control air headers. One SAC is capable of supplying both unit's station air and control air headers to maintain around 100 psig (assuming no demands from the polisher).</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; A.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if you believe that the ECACs cannot maintain control air headers around 85-90 psig when all SACs are lost.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if you believe that the ECACs cannot maintain control air headers around 85-90 psig when all SACs are lost.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if you believe that no SACs are running and both units Emergency Air Compressors (ECACs) have started and are maintaining both CA headers around the auto start setpoint of 85 to 90 psig. The Unit 1 ECAC (B CA header) is supplied from 1C 460 V Vital Bus and the Unit 2 ECAC (A CA header) is supplied from the 2C 460 V Vital Bus.</div>
