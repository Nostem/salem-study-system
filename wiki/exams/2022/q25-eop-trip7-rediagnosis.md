<span style="color:#60a5fa;font-size:14px;font-weight:600;">Q25 — EOP-TRIP-7 Rediagnosis</span>
<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">W E01 EK3.2 (3.0)</span>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br><br>
- Unit 2 has completed all actions in 2-EOP-LOSC-1, Faulted Steam Generator Isolation, to isolate a steam leak on 21 steam generator.<br>
- The crew has just transitioned to 2-EOP-TRIP-3, SI Termination.<br>
- The crew now reports the following plant conditions:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#x25CB; PZR level is lowering.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#x25CB; RCS pressure is lowering.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#x25CB; ALL SG pressures are stable.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#x25CB; 21 SG WR level is rising.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#x25CB; 2R19A, SG Blowdown, radiation monitor is in alarm.<br>
- The CRS is unsure of the transition to 2-EOP-TRIP-3 and enters 2-EOP-TRIP-7, Rediagnosis.<br>
<br>
In accordance with 2-EOP-TRIP-7, which ONE of the following procedures will the crew transition to NEXT?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 2-EOP-LOCA-1, Loss of Reactor Coolant</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 2-EOP-LOSC-1, Faulted Steam Generator Isolation</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> 2-EOP-TRIP-1, Reactor Trip or Safety Injection</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> 2-EOP-SGTR-1, Steam Generator Tube Rupture</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">&#x25B6; Show Answer &amp; Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#x2713; D. Correct.</strong> IAW 2-EOP-TRIP-7 and the conditions given in the stem, conditions are not symptoms of a faulted or a loss of RCS, and therefore, the next procedure to transition to would be 2-EOP-SGTR-1 due to rising SG levels and SG high radiation. EOP-TRIP-3 only transitions to EOP-LOCA-1, LOCA-2, IOP-6 &amp; 8 procedures and therefore, if the crew remains in EOP-TRIP-3 with the above condition, there will be no transition directed in the aforementioned EOPs to EOP-SGTR-1.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#x2717; A.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because EOP-TRIP-7 allows transition to EOP-LOCA-1 and the operator may believe based on lowering PZR level and pressure that a LOCA is in progress and transition to 2-EOP-LOCA-1.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#x2717; B.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because EOP-TRIP-7 allows transition to EOP-LOSC-1 and the operator may incorrectly believe that EOP-TRIP-7 will direct you back to procedure in effect which was EOP-LOSC-1.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#x2717; C.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible because the operator may incorrectly believe that EOP-TRIP-7 will direct you to EOP-TRIP-1 where diagnosis are performed for symptoms of a LOCA, SGTR and LOSC.</div>
