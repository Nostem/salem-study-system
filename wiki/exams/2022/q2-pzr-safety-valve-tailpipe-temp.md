<span style="color:#60a5fa;font-size:14px;font-weight:600;">Q2 — PZR Safety Valve Tailpipe Temperature</span>
<span style="font-size:10px;padding:2px 8px;background:#0d1220;color:#6b7280;border-radius:3px;">APE08 AK2.02 (2.7)</span>

<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br><br>
• A manual reactor trip and SI have been actuated on Unit 1.<br>
• A PZR safety valve has been stuck slightly open for ten minutes.<br>
• RCS pressure is 1900 psig and slowly lowering.<br>
• PZR vapor space temperature is 630 °F and stable.<br>
• PRT level is 80% and slowly rising.<br>
• PRT pressure is 35 psig and slowly rising.<br>
• Containment pressure is 4 psig and slowly rising.<br><br>
Currently, safety valve tailpipe temperature indication should be reading approximately:
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 282 °F</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 265 °F</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> 230 °F</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> 200 °F</div>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">&#10003; A. Correct.</strong> The safety valve leak is a throttling process that follows a constant enthalpy process across the valve. The RCS pressure of 1900 psig (1915 psia) equates to a saturated vapor enthalpy (hf) of 1140 BTU/lb. Using the Mollier diagram and following the 1140 BTU/lb enthalpy line (horizontal) until it intersects with the pressure line for the PRT of 35 psig (50 psia). Then follow up the 50 psia line to determine the temperature which will be approx. 282 °F.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; B.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if the applicant misapplies the Mollier diagram by throttling to PRT pressure of 35 psia and not 50 psia (35 psig).</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; C.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if the applicant misapplies the Mollier diagram by throttling to containment pressure of 4 psig (19 psia). The current conditions support the PRT rupture disc remaining intact, therefore this is incorrect.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">&#10007; D.</strong> Incorrect. Plausible if the applicant misapplies the Mollier diagram by throttling to the saturation curve.</div>
