<strong>Given:</strong><br>
• LOCA per EOP-LOCA-6<br>
• All ECCS pumps running<br>
• Suspected SI pump cold leg discharge leak<br>
• RCS 1700 psig steady<br><br>
What indicates a leak and which valve isolates it?

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> RCS at 1700 psig is above SI pump shutoff head (1520 psig). Flow indicated on SI pumps means a leak exists on cold leg(s). Per EOP-LOCA-6 step 2, 2SJ135 Cold Leg Discharge Valve is closed to isolate SI pump cold leg leak.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> SJ49s are used to isolate RHR cold legs, not SI cold legs. Flow is the correct indicator but the isolation valve is wrong.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> SJ49s isolate RHR cold legs, not SI cold legs. SI pump discharge pressure alone does not confirm a leak — flow is the indicator since RCS pressure is above shutoff head.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> SI pump discharge pressure alone does not confirm a leak — flow is the indicator since RCS pressure is above shutoff head. 2SJ135 is the correct valve but the wrong confirming indication.</div>
