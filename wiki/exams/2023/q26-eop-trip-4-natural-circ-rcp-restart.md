<strong>Given:</strong><br>
• Unit 2 experienced a LOOP<br>
• Crew is performing EOP-TRIP-4, Natural Circulation<br>
• Power has been restored to 4KV Group Buses<br><br>
How many RCPs are started and why?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> One RCP; prevents two phase flow in the RCS.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> Two RCPs; prevents two phase flow in the RCS.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> Two RCPs; permits faster cooldown with less potential for upper head voiding.</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> One RCP; permits faster cooldown with less potential for upper head voiding.</div>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> Per EOP-TRIP-4 bases Note N1-3: RCPs are started whenever possible. Forced convection permits faster cooldown with less potential for upper head voiding. RCP priority order: 23, 21+22 or 21+24, 21 (to provide normal PZR spray). Single pump operation is preferred in the loop providing best spray.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Incorrect. "Prevents two phase flow" is the reason for stopping an RCP during a SBLOCA, not the reason for starting one during natural circulation cooldown. The correct reason is faster cooldown with less potential for upper head voiding.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect. Two RCPs would only be started if 23 RCP is unavailable (to provide spray using 21+22 or 21+24). The "prevents two phase flow" reasoning is the basis for stopping RCPs during a SBLOCA, not for starting them during natural circulation.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect. While the reason (faster cooldown, less head voiding) is correct, only one RCP is preferred. Two RCPs are only started if 23 is unavailable for spray. Note N1-1 allows two RCPs for better spray if 23 unavailable.</div>
