<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ D. Correct.</strong> Rising backpressure makes LP turbines less efficient, resulting in lowering generator MWe output. Operational concern is overpressurization and damage to the main condenser/LP turbine.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> Condenser diff temp is not the best confirming indication — lowering MWe is the primary indicator of reduced LP turbine efficiency.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Rising diff temp is the wrong indication. Concern is LP turbine damage, not condensate pump cavitation.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Lowering MWe is the correct indication, but the concern is LP turbine damage not condensate pump cavitation.</div>
