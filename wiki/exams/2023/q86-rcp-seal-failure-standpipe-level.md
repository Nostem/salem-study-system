<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
• Salem Unit 2 is at 100% power<br><br>
<strong>Then:</strong><br>
• Console Alarm, STANDPIPE LEVEL-LO, is now LIT<br>
• The RO reports the following for 21 RCP:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;- Seal Leak-off recorder indicates 1.5 gpm and steady<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;- Seal Injection Flow indicates 8.5 gpm<br><br>
Which of the following completes both statements?<br><br>
1. Which RCP seal failed?<br>
2. What action(s) will the CRS direct the crew?<br><br>
Note: ARP = Alarm Response Procedure<br>
S2.OP-AB.RCP-0001, Reactor Coolant Pump Abnormality

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> The conditions in the stem describes a #3 seal leak. Standpipe level low is a primary indicator that the #3 seal is leaking only since the #1 seal leakoff flow is steady at 1.5 gpm which is normal conditions for an intact #2 seal. The crew will take action per the console ARP and perform a makeup to clear the low standpipe level alarm and initiate S2.OP-ST.RC-0008 for RCS leak rate. The CRS could enter S2.OP-AB.RCP-0001 based on the console alarm, but the AB will not provide any direction for a leaking #3 seal.</div>

Part 2 is incorrect. Plausible because this is an action in S2.OP-AB.RCP-0001 for a leaking or degraded #2 seal.</div>
