<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> Per EOP-LOCA-5 step 23, during RCS depressurization upper head voiding CAN be expected. Hotter liquid in upper head flashes to steam, displaces water into PZR causing rapidly rising PZR level. Monitor PZR level to stop depressurization before going solid.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> CETs may increase but EOP-LOCA-5 specifically directs monitoring PZR level for this condition, not CETs.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Upper head voiding IS expected during RCS depressurization with no RCPs running. The monitoring parameter (PZR level) is correct but the voiding expectation is wrong.</div>
