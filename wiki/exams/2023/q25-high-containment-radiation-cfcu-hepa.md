In LOW speed, dampers realign flow through HEPA filters (bypassing roughing filters) to reduce radioactivity inside containment.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ A.</strong> High speed is normal operation with roughing filters. LOW speed is required for HEPA filtration to reduce containment radioactivity.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> CFCUs can run high speed but dampers only realign to route flow through HEPA filters when in low speed.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> LOW speed is the correct speed, but flow goes through HEPA filters not roughing filters. The damper realignment in low speed bypasses roughing filters.</div>
