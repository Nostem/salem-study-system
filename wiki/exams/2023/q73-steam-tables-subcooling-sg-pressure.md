<div style="font-size:13px;color:#9ca3af;margin-bottom:14px;line-height:1.7;">
<strong>Given:</strong><br>
- Salem Unit 2 is shutdown.<br>
- RCS pressure is 1805 psig and stable.<br>
- Core decay heat is being removed via the Steam Generators (SGs) using the MS10s, Atmospheric Dump Valves<br><br>
What is the HIGHEST pressure in the SGs to obtain a MINIMUM 100 degrees F subcooling margin in the reactor coolant leaving the SGs (assume the reactor coolant leaves the SGs at the SG saturation temperature)?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> 790 psig</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> 805 psig</div>

<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ B. Correct.</strong> At 1805 psig or 1820 psia the RCS saturation temperature is 623 degrees F. Therefore, to obtain a 100 degrees F subcooling margin in the RCS loop cold legs, temperature must be 100 degrees F lower than 623 degrees F (which is equal to 523 degrees F). Because a negligible temperature difference exist across the steam generator tubes, steam generator temperature must also be approximately 523 degrees F. Because core heat is being removed by the SGs, the SGs are a saturated system. Therefore, to determine correct SG pressure to obtain 100 degrees F subcooling margin, one must determine the saturation pressure at 523 degrees F, which is approximately 820 psia (which is equal to approximately 805 psig).</div>
