<strong>Location:</strong> Classroom (Administrative)<br>
<strong>Time-Critical:</strong> No<br>
<strong>Alternate Path:</strong> No<br>
<strong>Estimated Time:</strong> 20 minutes<br><br>
<strong>Initial Conditions:</strong><br>
- Salem Unit 1 is experiencing a LBLOCA<br>
- Transfer to Cold Leg Recirculation has been established<br>
- An operator venting 11 RHR Pump is injured and needs assistance to exit the RCA<br>
- Emergency Coordinator (EC) authorized one volunteer to attempt lifesaving task<br>
- Rad Pro predicts dose of 15-20 REM given general radiation levels in the area of the injured person<br><br>
<strong>Initiating Cue:</strong><br>
The EC directs you to perform the following:<br>
Evaluate and select the most preferred volunteer from the list of available volunteers per NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304, Operational Support Center (OSC) Radiation Protection Response.<br><br>
List of Available Volunteers:<br>
Volunteer 1: 46 year old male, newly hired Custodian. No prior radiation protection training.<br>
Volunteer 2: 27 year old female; informs you she is pregnant.<br>
Volunteer 3: 24 year old male, recently qualified NEO.<br>
Volunteer 4: 52 year old male, lifetime exposure 150 REM, including a Protecting Valuable Property dose of 15 REM at another facility.<br>
Volunteer 5: 49 year old male, works in Maintenance; informs you that he is on prescription medication that may cause dizziness.<br>
Volunteer 6: 42 year old female, not capable of becoming pregnant.<br>
Volunteer 7: 50 year old male, SRO at Salem. Recently removed from License duties due to a failed periodic written exam.
</div>

<div style="padding:10px 14px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #34d399;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:14px;font-size:13px;">
<strong style="color:#34d399;">Task Standard:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">The task is satisfactorily met when the applicant has; (1) determined that Volunteers 1-6 did NOT meet the requirements of NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304, AND (2) determines that Volunteer 7 does MEET the requirements of NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304 to perform lifesaving tasks. All documentation recorded to the reason why or why NOT is in accordance with the attached answer key.</span>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:12px;padding:10px;background:#1a1400;border-left:3px solid #fbbf24;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color:#fbbf24;">Emergency Exposure Volunteer Criteria (NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304):</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">
- Must be familiar with consequences of radiation exposure (Rad Worker trained)<br>
- Declared pregnant women SHALL NOT volunteer<br>
- Preference for volunteers over 45 years of age<br>
- Must not have received previous emergency exposure<br>
- Must be physically qualified (no disqualifying medications)<br>
- Not a female capable of reproduction (preference)
</span>
</div>

<div style="margin-top:12px;padding:10px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color:#22c55e;">Key Decision Point:</strong><br>
<span style="color:#c8cdd5;">The applicant must systematically evaluate each volunteer against NC.EP-EP.ZZ-0304 criteria and correctly eliminate Volunteers 1-6 for specific disqualifying reasons, then select Volunteer 7 as the only acceptable candidate. Note that Volunteer 7's failed periodic exam does NOT disqualify him — he is still a trained SRO familiar with radiation consequences.</span>
</div>
