<strong>Given:</strong><br>
• A small break LOCA has occurred<br><br>
Which RCS break location results in the highest peak clad temperature during a SBLOCA?
</div>

<div style="margin-bottom:14px;">
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>A.</strong> Cold leg</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>B.</strong> Hot leg</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>C.</strong> CRDM housing on reactor head</div>
<div style="padding:10px 14px;border-left:3px solid #1a2035;margin-bottom:4px;font-size:12px;color:#c8cdd5;background:#0d1220;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;"><strong>D.</strong> Pressurizer steam space</div>
</div>

<details>
<summary style="cursor:pointer;color:#60a5fa;font-size:12px;font-weight:600;padding:8px 0;">▶ Show Answer & Explanation</summary>
<div style="margin-top:10px;padding:16px;background:#0a0e17;border:1px solid #1a2035;border-radius:6px;font-size:12px;">
<div style="padding:8px 12px;background:#052e16;border-left:3px solid #22c55e;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:10px;color:#c8cdd5;"><strong style="color:#22c55e;">✓ A. Correct.</strong> A cold leg break results in the highest peak clad temperature during a SBLOCA. This is due to thermodynamics and the fact that ECCS pumps inject into the cold legs. With a cold leg break, injected ECCS water can be lost directly out the break before reaching the core, reducing the effectiveness of core cooling and resulting in the highest PCT.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ B.</strong> Incorrect. A hot leg break results in the highest loss of flow but does not produce the highest peak clad temperature. ECCS injection into cold legs is more effective at reaching the core with a hot leg break since the break is downstream of the core.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ C.</strong> Incorrect. A CRDM housing break on the reactor head is an analyzed SBLOCA scenario but does not produce the worst PCT. The break size is limited and flow paths still allow effective ECCS delivery to the core.</div>
<div style="padding:8px 12px;border-left:3px solid #f87171;border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;margin-bottom:6px;color:#9ca3af;"><strong style="color:#f87171;">✗ D.</strong> Incorrect. A pressurizer steam space break is an analyzed SBLOCA scenario but does not produce the worst PCT. The steam space break depressurizes the PZR but ECCS can still effectively deliver coolant to the core.</div>
