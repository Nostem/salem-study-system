# LS-AA-119-1003 — Calculating Work Hours

## Purpose

Establishes requirements for calculating and enforcing work hour limits for licensed operators and safety-related personnel in accordance with 10 CFR 26 Subpart I (Fatigue Management).

## Non-Outage Work Hour Limits

| Rule | Limit | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| Continuous 24-hour period | Cannot exceed <span class="hi-exam">16 hours</span> | LS-AA-119-1003 |
| Continuous 48-hour period | Cannot exceed <span class="hi-exam">26 hours</span> | LS-AA-119-1003 |
| Continuous 168-hour period (7 days) | Cannot exceed <span class="hi-exam">72 hours</span> | LS-AA-119-1003 |
| Minimum break in any 9-day period | <span class="hi-exam">34 hours continuous</span> | LS-AA-119-1003 |
| Minimum break between shifts | <span class="hi-exam">10 hours continuous</span> | LS-AA-119-1003 |

<div class="callout callout-jpm">
<div class="callout-label">JPM — 2023 SRO-A1.b</div>
Work hour rule evaluation: Operator 1 eliminated for not having a <span class="hi-exam">34-hour break in any 9 calendar day period</span> (only 33 hours). Operator 3 eliminated for exceeding <span class="hi-exam">72 hours in a 7-day period</span> (73 hours total). Only Operator 2 meets all five work hour requirements.
