# RP-AA-4000 — Personnel Conduct in Radiologically Controlled Areas

## Purpose

Administrative procedure governing personnel conduct and dosimetry requirements in Radiologically Controlled Areas (RCAs), including requirements for self-reading dosimeter monitoring frequency and alarm response actions.

## Key Actions / Information

### Self-Reading Dosimeter Monitoring Frequency

| Area Type | Monitoring Frequency |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Radiation Area | Every 30 minutes |
| **HIGH Radiation Area** | **Every 15 minutes** |

### Dosimeter Alarm Response

<div class="callout callout-exam">
<div class="callout-label">Exam — 2019 Q44</div>
Per RP-AA-4000, self-reading dosimeters should be read approximately every <span class="hi-exam">15 minutes</span> while working in a HIGH Radiation Area (<span class="hi-trap">30 minutes is for a Radiation Area — different from a HIGH Radiation Area</span>). If an unexpected self-reading dosimeter comes into alarm, the worker is REQUIRED to <span class="hi-exam">exit the area immediately</span> and notify RP personnel. <span class="hi-trap">Trap: the worker must EXIT the area, not just go to a low dose area within the HRA. Exiting is the required action before RP verification of the alarm.</span>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-exam">
<div class="callout-label">Exam — 2018 Q44</div>
Confirms same facts tested in 2019 Q44: self-reading dosimeter monitoring frequency in a HIGH Radiation Area = <span class="hi-exam">every 15 minutes</span> (not 30 minutes — that is for a standard Radiation Area). On unexpected dosimeter alarm, NEO must <span class="hi-exam">exit the HRA immediately</span> and notify RP — NOT just go to a low dose area within the HRA.
</div>
